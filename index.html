Bisection Method

Requires an interval [a,b] where f(a) and f(b) have opposite signs. Guaranteed to converge but relatively slow.

False Position Method

Similar to bisection but uses linear interpolation. Usually faster than bisection.

Fixed Point Iteration

Requires rewriting f(x)=0 as x=g(x). Convergence depends on the choice of g(x).

Newton-Raphson Method

Uses derivative information for faster convergence. Requires a good initial guess.

Secant Method

Similar to Newton-Raphson but doesn't require derivative calculation. Needs two initial guesses.
